Happiness and Gross National Product per Cpaita


Metric: Researchers “extended the GDP time series from 2017 to 2018 using country specific forecasts of real GDP growth from the OECD Economic Outlook No. 104 (Edition November 2018) and the World Bank’s Global Economic Prospects (Last Updated: 06/07/2018), after adjustment for population growth.” – WHR 2019, pg. 25

Potentially unsurprisingly, the scatter plot provides the linear relationship between happiness scores and an independent variable within the entire dataset. With the exception of a few outliers, who earn less than their groupings on average, there is nearly a 1 to 1 ratio in increasing happiness and larger GDP per capita. It is, however, noteworthy that the three nations with the highest GDP have a high happiness score, but only one scores above 7. On average, it appears that money can buy happiness, but it certainly isn’t the only factor.
