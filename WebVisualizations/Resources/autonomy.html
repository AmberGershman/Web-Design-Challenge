Happiness and Social Freedoms


Question: Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?

Researchers explored the question of autonomy and the results are surprisingly unclear. There is no definite correlation, although the highest ranked nations also were more likely answer that the were satisfied with their personal freedoms. In fact, all respondents in the highest-ranking countries scored above 35% in autonomy, with most above 50%. Comparatively, roughly half of lowest-ranking countries also scored below 35%.

Ultimately, the notion of personal freedoms has little impact on whether a citizen considers herself happy. With a country like the US placing an enormous premium on autonomy, this plot undermines the rally cry that others “hate us for our freedom”.
