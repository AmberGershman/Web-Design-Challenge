Happiness and Health


Metric: “The time series of healthy life expectancy at birth are constructed based on data from the World Health Organization (WHO) Global Health Observatory data repository, with data available for 2005, 2010, 2015, and 2016.” WHR 2019, pg. 25

Again, researchers looked to a quantifiable measurement in relation to a nation’s self-identified happiness scores and again, this plots a more liner path. Lower scoring countries were substantially more likely to have truncated life expectancy ages, while higher scoring countries enjoyed higher life expectancies. It is notable that the impact of health on happiness is not as demonstrably linked to a happiness score than GDP per capita. In fact, the life expectancy of countries with a happiness score between 4-5 was substantially more varied (between 0.0 and 0.9) than the nations with happiness scores below 4 (0.1 and 0.6).

The benefits of earning a fair and decent wage and anticipating a long and healthy life are clearly correlative to a greater happiness score. These basic measures speak to the trust and stability one has, both within their family and their very existence. It is a completely logical extension that those that have not been afforded the advantages of comfort and ease equally do not enjoy the same contentment in life.
